Why do people tend to deep clean on spring?

The practice of deep cleaning is said to have its roots in the 19th century, as people capitalized on the fresh, warm spring air to help eliminate dust and musty odors from their homes after the winter season. According to the 2023 Statista survey in the United States, 74 percent of respondents engage in annual spring cleaning. Spring symbolizes renewal and rebirth, making it a natural time for people to declutter, organize, and refresh their living spaces.

Recent studies have shown that around 86% of adults in the US use the start of spring as motivation to clean and freshen up their homes. After keeping windows closed during the winter, spring provides an opportunity to ventilate the home and improve indoor air quality by dusting, vacuuming, and airing out rooms. Among those who spring clean, survey findings show Americans focus their targeted hygiene practices on the kitchen (64%), bedrooms (61%) and bathrooms (59%). When choosing their cleaning products, Americans who spring clean rank effectiveness (44%) as the most important, followed by multi-purpose (18%) and cost (12%).
